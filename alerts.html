Alerts

Alerts provide contextual feedback messages for typical user actions, as well as alerting them to important information they may need to know. Information and warning messages usually appear at the top of a page, under the Success and error messages might appear above a form. Use the appropriate color to match the purpose of your message. 

Guidelines

  • Keep alerts to 55-75 characters including spaces
  • Use only one alert per page
  • Alerts should have a time limit and be removed promptly

Example alerts

Success:

Well done. You successfully filled out this form.

When to use: Success messages such as properly submitting a form

Warning:

Important! Choose an in-network primary care provider to help lower your healthcare costs.

When to use: Warning messages such as letting members know they need a primary care physician before seeing a doctor

Danger:

Error: Missing info

When to use: Error messages such as filling out a form incorrectly; when we won’t let user progress any further without providing the requested info, or when user is being given a chance to make a decision that is irreversible, hard to change, or has legal implications (for example, granting permissions for claims access).

More information on error messages

When users get an error message, they are probably feeling confused, stressed, frustrated, even angry.

Tips

  • Offer a solution or next step.
  • Be straightforward. Explain what's going on right away.
  • Be calm. Don’t use exclamation points or alarming words like “alert” or "immediately."
  • Be serious. Don’t joke around with people who are frustrated.
